Easy Sunday

‘Peyton on Sunday Morning’

In American culture, as currently typified by the riff on this for DirectTV, ‘Peyton on Sunday Morning’, there is no real schedule. The Commodores would have understood the religious aspect but the message is that unlike ‘the work week’ + Saturday/ chore day, Sunday is a day of rest - or at least without schedule.

If you read it carefully, assuming you can read it from the US cultural context,
it is so very clear.

The song speaks about the relief of ending a really bad - or at least really difficult - relationship where the speaker (Male) has paid his dues - worked hard to please, and now he realizes it’s time to go. There is a freedom in just walking away - ‘in laying my burdens down’ - and moving on into an undefined future. If you read it carefully, assuming you can read it from the US cultural context, it is so very clear. And if you have ever decided you are just too tired to push that rock up the hill anymore and let it go - you will understand this. The gentle tone of the accompaniment underscores the easy feeling. You might try ‘Walk away Renee’ as a similar vibration.
